## Segment 1: SaaS Founders & Indie Hackers

### Profile
- **Who:** Solo founders, small dev teams, indie hackers
- **Age:** 25-45
- **Tech level:** High (can edit code)
- **Budget:** $100-300 for tools that save time
- **Decision speed:** Fast (hours to days)

### Pain Points
1. "Design isn't my strength"
2. "I need to validate fast, not spend weeks on a landing page"
3. "I want to look as legit as well-funded startups"
4. "Most templates look generic and dated"

### What They Want
- Modern, SaaS-specific sections (features, pricing, testimonials)
- Dark mode option
- Animation and micro-interactions
- Easy to customize colors/fonts
- SEO and performance optimized
- Integrations (analytics, email capture)

### Template Requirements
- **Style:** Clean, minimal, modern (think Linear, Vercel, Stripe)
- **Sections:** Hero, features grid, how-it-works, pricing table, testimonials, FAQ, CTA
- **Tech:** Next.js, Tailwind, Framer Motion
- **CMS needs:** Pricing tiers, feature lists, testimonials

### Marketing Channels
- Product Hunt
- Indie Hackers
- Twitter/X (#buildinpublic)
- Reddit (r/SaaS, r/startups, r/webdev)
- Hacker News

### Template Name Ideas
- **"SaaS Launch"**
- **"Indie Stack"**
- **"Ship Pro"**

## Segment 2: Coaches, Consultants & Course Creators

### Profile
- **Who:** Life coaches, business consultants, online educators
- **Age:** 30-55
- **Tech level:** Low (scared of code)
- **Budget:** $100-200 for professional presence
- **Decision speed:** Medium (days to weeks)

### Pain Points
1. "I'm not technical at all"
2. "I need to look professional to charge premium prices"
3. "My current website doesn't convert"
4. "I don't have time to learn web design"

### What They Want
- Personal branding focus (photo, bio, story)
- Lead capture and email signup
- Service/offer showcase
- Social proof (testimonials, as-seen-in logos)
- Booking/calendar integration capability
- Mobile-perfect (their clients browse on phones)

### Template Requirements
- **Style:** Warm, personal, trustworthy (not cold/corporate)
- **Sections:** Hero with photo, about/story, services, results/testimonials, lead magnet, contact
- **Tech:** Simple, visual CMS critical
- **CMS needs:** Services, testimonials, blog posts, images

### Marketing Channels
- Instagram
- LinkedIn
- Facebook groups (coaching communities)
- YouTube (tutorial content)
- Pinterest

### Template Name Ideas
- **"Coach Pro"**
- **"Expert Page"**
- **"Authority"**

## Segment 3: Creative Agencies & Freelancers

### Profile
- **Who:** Design agencies, freelance designers, dev shops
- **Age:** 25-40
- **Tech level:** High
- **Budget:** $150-300 (business expense)
- **Decision speed:** Fast (need for client work)

### Pain Points
1. "I need to deliver fast without sacrificing quality"
2. "Clients want custom but don't have custom budgets"
3. "I need something I can reskin for different clients"
4. "My own agency site needs to be impressive"

### What They Want
- High design quality (they have taste)
- Easy to customize and rebrand
- Portfolio showcase capability
- Case study layouts
- Team/about sections
- Contact and inquiry forms

### Template Requirements
- **Style:** Bold, creative, portfolio-focused
- **Sections:** Hero (statement piece), work showcase, services, about/team, process, contact
- **Tech:** Clean code they can extend
- **CMS needs:** Portfolio items, team members, services

### Marketing Channels
- Dribbble
- Behance
- Design Twitter
- Agency directories
- Web design blogs

### Template Name Ideas
- **"Studio"**
- **"Agency Pro"**
- **"Folio"**

## Segment 4: Local Businesses

### Profile
- **Who:** Restaurants, salons, gyms, dentists, contractors
- **Age:** 35-60
- **Tech level:** Very low
- **Budget:** $50-150
- **Decision speed:** Slow (not a priority until it is)

### Pain Points
1. "I don't have time for this"
2. "I just need something simple that looks good"
3. "My competitor has a nice website"
4. "I don't want to pay monthly fees forever"

### What They Want
- Location and contact info prominent
- Hours of operation
- Services/menu display
- Photo gallery
- Reviews integration (Google, Yelp)
- Mobile-first (customers search on phones)

### Template Requirements
- **Style:** Clean, trustworthy, industry-appropriate
- **Sections:** Hero with CTA, services/menu, about, gallery, reviews, location/contact
- **Tech:** Dead simple setup
- **CMS needs:** Services, images, hours, contact info

### Marketing Channels
- Google Ads (local)
- Facebook Ads (local targeting)
- Local business associations
- Word of mouth
- Google My Business integration

### Template Name Ideas
- **"Local Pro"**
- **"Storefront"**
- **"Neighborhood"**

## Segment 5: Product Launchers

### Profile
- **Who:** Anyone launching a physical product, app, book
- **Age:** Varies
- **Tech level:** Medium
- **Budget:** $75-150 (campaign expense)
- **Decision speed:** Fast (launch date driven)

### Pain Points
1. "I need something up fast for my launch"
2. "I need to capture emails before launch"
3. "I want to build hype"
4. "The product is the star, not the website"

### What They Want
- Product-focused hero (big images/video)
- Coming soon / waitlist mode
- Email capture
- Countdown timer option
- Feature highlights
- Press kit / media section

### Template Requirements
- **Style:** Product-forward, high-impact
- **Sections:** Hero with product, features, specs, waitlist, FAQ, press
- **Tech:** Flexible for different product types
- **CMS needs:** Product info, features, FAQ, launch date

### Marketing Channels
- Product Hunt
- Kickstarter/Indiegogo communities
- Industry-specific forums
- Influencer marketing

### Template Name Ideas
- **"Launch"**
- **"Product Drop"**
- **"Reveal"**

## Priority Template Builds

Based on market size, willingness to pay, and our competitive advantages (shaders, quality):

### Must Build (Top 3)

1. **SaaS/Startup Template**
   - Highest willingness to pay
   - Appreciates shader effects
   - Active online communities for marketing
   - Can handle technical setup

2. **Coach/Consultant Template**
   - Large market
   - Clear pain point (need credibility)
   - Will pay for "premium" feel
   - Low support burden if CMS is good

3. **Agency/Creative Template**
   - High design standards = willing to pay more
   - Repeat purchases for client work
   - Word-of-mouth in design communities

### Nice to Have (Later)

4. **Local Business Template** (volume play, lower margin)
5. **Product Launch Template** (seasonal, specific use case)
