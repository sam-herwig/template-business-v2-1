# Local Business Website Template Market Research

*Research compiled January 30, 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

**The Opportunity:** 27% of US small businesses still don't have a website. Of those that do, many use outdated or generic templates. Local service businesses (restaurants, contractors, salons, gyms) have specific needs that generic templates don't address well.

**Market Size:**
- Website template market: **$1.3B** (2023), growing 7.5% annually ‚Üí **$2.2B by 2028**
- 45% of small businesses use templates as their primary solution
- Templates save businesses **50-70%** vs custom development

**Key Insight:** Local businesses need industry-specific features (booking, menus, quote forms) but most templates are generic. There's a gap between cheap generic templates ($17-40) and expensive custom development ($2,000-$9,000).

## Market Sizing by Vertical

### üçΩÔ∏è Restaurants & Food Service

**Market Size:**
- **427,000** SMB restaurants in the US (Statista 2020)
- 90% of US restaurants are small businesses (<50 employees)
- **69%** have a website ‚Üí **31% don't** (133,000 restaurants without sites)
- 77% of diners visit restaurant website before deciding to eat there

**The Problem:**
- 70% of diners have been **discouraged** from visiting due to a bad website
- Generic templates lack menu integration, online ordering, reservations
- Most restaurants can't afford $2K-$9K custom development

**Must-Have Features:**
1. **Online menu** (searchable, mobile-friendly, dietary filters)
2. **Online ordering** integration (Toast, Square, GloriaFood)
3. **Reservation system** (OpenTable, Resy, or built-in)
4. **Gallery** (food, ambiance, team)
5. **Location + hours** (Google Maps embed)
6. **Mobile-first** (63% of searches are mobile)

**Competitors:**
- Toast Websites (bundled with POS, restaurant-specific)
- BentoBox (premium, $99-$199/mo)
- ThemeForest restaurant templates: 3,328 options, $17-40 each
- GloriaFood (free ordering widget, basic site builder)

**Pricing Opportunity:**
- Gap between $40 templates (generic) and $99/mo managed solutions
- **Sweet spot: $79-$149 one-time** for restaurant-specific template

### üî® Contractors & Construction

**Market Size:**
- **3.87 million** construction businesses in US (2024)
- Over 50% have **fewer than 5 employees**
- Includes: general contractors, plumbers, electricians, HVAC, roofers, landscapers

**The Problem:**
- Most contractor websites look outdated or unprofessional
- Trust is critical ‚Äî homeowners vet contractors online before calling
- Generic templates don't include quote request forms, service areas, licensing info

**Must-Have Features:**
1. **Quote/estimate request form** (capture leads)
2. **Services page** (with pricing ranges if applicable)
3. **Project portfolio/gallery** (before/after photos)
4. **Testimonials** (trust signals are essential)
5. **Service area** (map or list of cities/zip codes)
6. **Licensing & insurance info** (credibility)
7. **Click-to-call** (mobile-first)

**Competitors:**
- Squarespace contractor templates (limited selection)
- Wix contractor templates (generic, need heavy customization)
- Dazzly (new entrant, focused on trades)
- WordPress themes on ThemeForest ($39-59)
- Contractor-specific: Jeremyfenn, ContractorWeb

**Pricing Opportunity:**
- Most contractors don't want to learn Squarespace/Wix
- Value proposition: "Professional site in 10 minutes"
- **Sweet spot: $79-$129 one-time** for contractor template

### üíá Hair Salons & Barbershops

**Market Size:**
- **1.2 million** hair salons and barbershops in US
- 86% are independent (non-chain)
- Beauty services market: $46.2B annually

**The Problem:**
- Booking is the #1 pain point ‚Äî clients want online scheduling
- Many salons rely only on Instagram (no website)
- Generic templates don't integrate with salon booking software

**Must-Have Features:**
1. **Online booking** integration (Vagaro, Fresha, Booksy, GlossGenius)
2. **Services + pricing** (clear menu of offerings)
3. **Stylist profiles** (with specialties and availability)
4. **Gallery** (portfolio of work, Instagram feed)
5. **Contact + location** (hours, parking info)
6. **Gift cards** (if supported)

**Booking Platform Integrations (Critical):**
- Fresha (free, popular)
- Vagaro ($25-85/mo)
- Booksy ($30/mo)
- GlossGenius ($24-48/mo)
- Square Appointments (free-$69/mo)

**Competitors:**
- GlossGenius includes basic website
- Squarespace salon templates (6-8 options)
- Wix salon templates (10+ options)
- Dedicated: SalonWebsites.com, BeautyPros

**Pricing Opportunity:**
- Salon owners often not tech-savvy
- Need done-for-you feel with booking integration
- **Sweet spot: $79-$99 one-time** (emphasize booking integration)

- **Sweet spot: $99-$149 one-time** (highlight software integrations)

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Target Customers

**Primary:** Small business owners who:
- Currently have no website or an outdated one
- Can't afford $2K+ custom development
- Don't have time to learn Squarespace/Wix from scratch
- Need industry-specific features (booking, menus, quotes)

**Secondary:** Web designers/agencies who:
- Build sites for local businesses
- Want quality starting points
- Need faster turnaround

### Channels

1. **SEO/Content**
   - "Restaurant website template"
   - "Contractor website builder"
   - "Salon website with booking"
   - Comparison posts: "Best templates for [industry]"

2. **Paid Ads**
   - Google Ads: Industry + template keywords
   - Facebook/Instagram: Target small business owners by industry

3. **Partnerships**
   - Booking software companies (Fresha, Vagaro, etc.)
   - POS companies (Toast, Square)
   - Local business associations

4. **Product Hunt / AppSumo**
   - Launch bundle for early traction
